# Meeting Prep Template

## Suggested Flow

1. **Understand** — Define the meeting purpose and constraints
   - What's the meeting about? (decision, update, brainstorm, review?)
   - Who's attending? What are their roles and interests?
   - How long is the meeting?
   - What needs to happen by the end? (a decision, alignment, next steps?)

2. **Gather** — Collect materials and information
   - Find relevant documents, data, reports
   - Collect context on discussion topics
   - Identify any pre-decisions or dependencies
   - Check mnem for prior meetings or decisions on this topic
   - Note any sensitive issues or potential conflicts

3. **Organize** — Structure the agenda
   - Order topics by priority (most important first)
   - Allocate time per item (total must fit meeting duration)
   - Assign owners to each agenda item
   - Flag which items need pre-read vs. can be presented live
   - Note desired outcome per item (decision, discussion, update)

4. **Review** — Check readiness
   - Does the agenda cover all stated goals?
   - Are materials complete and accessible?
   - Are time allocations realistic?
   - Is there a clear desired outcome for the meeting?
   - Should any items be moved to a follow-up?

5. **Package** — Prepare and distribute
   - Finalize the agenda document
   - Attach or link pre-read materials
   - Send to attendees with clear expectations
   - Note any pre-work attendees should complete

## Meeting-Prep Success Criteria

- [ ] Agenda is specific (not just "discuss X" — "decide whether X")
- [ ] Time allocations add up to meeting duration (with buffer)
- [ ] Every agenda item has a clear desired outcome
- [ ] Pre-read materials are identified and accessible
- [ ] Attendees know what's expected of them before the meeting

## Tips

- Start with the end: what should be different after this meeting?
- Time-box aggressively — meetings expand to fill their slot
- If an item doesn't need group discussion, take it offline
- Include "parking lot" time for unexpected items
- Send the agenda at least 24 hours before (48 for complex topics)
- For decision meetings: share proposals before the meeting, not during
- Check for prior `MakeMe:Decision` nodes that might be relevant
